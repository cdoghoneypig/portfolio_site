Edtech Music App

Client requested a new user experience for their music training app. A dozen in depth interviews with musicians and subject matter experts in music tech, usability testing with the current app, and research into the very competitive ear training app space gave us a strong sense of direction. However, no one was sure whether to move towards a game or keep it as a trainer.

To break the deadlock, we coded two prototypes: a Game Version and a Trainer. Testing and iterating these prototypes, we were able to deliver the client rudimentary empirical data and a checklist of incentive schemes that would help keep users hooked and practicing their music.

  • Role: Design & Code
  • Client: Awasu Design
  • Tools: Survey, interviews, Sketch, Processing, usability testing
  • Duration: 2 weeks
  • Year: 2016
An explorable world motivated users to continue training in the gamified prototype. This was one layer of motivation among many.
Screens to track player progress, compete with friends via social media, and award badges for accomplishments.
Gameplay in the original is nice to look at but actually quite confusing for players. Within thirty seconds, many said "I'm bad at this," losing confidence as musicians just because of weak UX.
Key elements of the app can be seen as options within categories, such as input buttons and target notes. This charting techniques allowed us to more easily experiment with alternatives.
The trainer prototype showed that fairly simple visual and audio cues could motivate the player to push through their intial boredom with ear training. No one likes conditioning; they like the results.
